A moon is defined to be a celestial body that makes an orbit around a planet, including the eight major planets, dwarf planets, and minor planets. A moon may also be referred to as a natural satellite, although to differentiate it from other astronomical bodies orbiting another body. In our solar system, we have more than 200 moon, but the most known ones are:

  1. The Earth's Moon

  2. Jupiter's moons, the Galilean Moons

    • Ganymede
    • Io
    • Europa
    • Callisto
